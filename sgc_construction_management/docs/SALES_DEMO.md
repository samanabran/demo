# SGC Construction Management — Sales Demo

> A short, friendly walkthrough. The goal isn't to show every button —
> it's to surface the small daily pains that quietly cost money, and show
> how the day-to-day work removes them.

**Demo length:** ~12 minutes. **Tone:** calm, practical, "this is your Monday morning, but easier."

---

## Open with the feeling, not the feature

> "Most teams don't lose money on the big disaster. They lose it in the
> small gaps — the variation nobody logged, the certificate that sat in
> someone's inbox, the cost overrun everyone *felt* but couldn't prove
> until month-end. Let me show you those gaps closing."

Then open the **Executive Dashboard**. Don't explain it. Just let them look.

---

## The five pains they live with (but rarely say out loud)

### 1. "I find out a project is bleeding *after* it's already bled."
**The hidden pain:** cost and schedule slip silently. By the time it reaches a report, the damage is locked in.

**Show:** the Dashboard's **Project Health Matrix** and **Critical Alerts** — "Actual 28% vs planned 74%, 46 days delay."

> "You're seeing this today, not at month-end. Three projects flagged
> critical — before the client calls you about it."

---

### 2. "Variations slip through the cracks."
**The hidden pain:** scope changes get agreed verbally on site, never formally logged, and never billed. Pure margin, gone.

**Show:** **Construction → Documents → Variation Orders** — add the agreed change with its amount and status (it defaults to the VO category). Then open the project and **Print → VO Register** to see them all totalled.

> "Every change is captured the moment it happens. Nothing walks off the
> job unbilled."

---

### 3. "Billing the client is a week of spreadsheet archaeology."
**The hidden pain:** preparing an interim payment certificate / RA bill means rebuilding numbers by hand, every cycle. Slow cash, tired QS.

**Show:** open an **RA Billing** record → **Print → RA Billing Certificate** (clean PDF, AED, ready to send). Then the **Statement of Account** for the client.

> "What took your QS a day is now a button. Faster certificates, faster cash."

---

### 4. "I don't know what a project will *finish* at — only what it's spent."
**The hidden pain:** teams track cost-to-date, but the question that matters is cost-to-*complete*. Without it, you're driving by the rear-view mirror.

**Show:** open a project → **Print → Cost-to-Complete**, and **Print → Cash Flow Forecast** (these are project print-outs, not separate menus).

> "This is the number your bank and your board actually want — the
> projected outcome, not just history."

---

### 5. "Every report looks different and half of them are wrong."
**The hidden pain:** numbers in different formats, wrong currency, headers you can't read when printed.

**Show:** print two or three reports side by side — consistent layout, AED throughout, wide tables in landscape so nothing is cramped.

> "One source of truth. Every certificate looks like it came from the
> same company — because it did."

---

## The "day in the life" close (this is the whole pitch)

Walk one short loop, narrating as a site/QS person would actually work:

1. **Morning:** open Dashboard → see the one project that needs attention today.
2. **On site:** a variation is agreed → log it under **Documents → Variation Orders** with its amount (30 seconds); it flows straight onto the project's VO Register.
3. **Mid-month:** raise the RA Billing → print the certificate → send to client.
4. **Month-end:** Cost-to-Complete + Portfolio report → leadership sees the real position.

> "Notice what we *didn't* do: no spreadsheets, no chasing inboxes, no
> rebuilding numbers. The same work you do today — just without the leaks."

---

## One-line takeaways (leave these on screen)

- See trouble **before** it costs you.
- Capture **every** variation — bill **every** dirham.
- Certificates and statements in **one click**.
- Know where a project will **land**, not just where it's been.
- One consistent, board-ready set of reports.
