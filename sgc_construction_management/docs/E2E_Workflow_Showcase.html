Planning — BOQ & WBS Phases

5The Bill of Quantities — the rulebook for billing

The BOQ on Al Noor Tower lists every contract item with its quantity and unit rate. This becomes the ceiling that RA Billing lines are checked against — a billing line can never claim more cumulative quantity than the BOQ allows.

Main BOQ — Al Noor Tower, approved with civil, structural, electrical, and finishing lines

6WBS Phases — breaking the job into trackable pieces

Work Breakdown Structure phases carry their own planned cost, planned/actual dates, and progress percentage. The project's overall progress and RAG status are derived from these phases, not typed in by hand.

Quality Control on Marina Heights

7Three quality records, one real failure

A concrete cylinder test failed on columns L8–L12, a re-inspection is in progress on the remedial work order, and a minor safety item was already resolved. The failed check is what matters next.

Quality Checks — Marina Heights: 1 failed, 1 in-progress re-inspection, 1 resolved safety item

8Inside the failed inspection

The remarks and corrective action are logged directly on the record — honeycombing on columns L9 and L11, strength results below grade C40, demolition and recast required before re-inspection.

Concrete Cylinder Test — Columns L8-L12: result Fail, state Failed
Why this matters This single failed check, tied to the structural-frame WBS phase, is enough to stop a client invoice from being created for that phase — automatically, the moment someone tries. No manual cross-checking required.

The Billing Gate — Live Block

9An RA Bill, approved and ready — except it isn't

RA Bill No. 1 on Marina Heights is fully drafted and internally approved, covering the structural frame BOQ line. A finance user clicks Create Invoice.

RA Bill No. 1 — Structural Works (Quality Hold): state Approved, Create Invoice available

10The system stops it — live, in the UI

No silent failure, no swallowed exception — a clear validation dialog naming the exact quality check responsible, so the finance user knows precisely what to chase down.

Live error: "Cannot create invoice: blocked by failed quality check(s): Concrete Cylinder Test - Columns L8-L12"
Scoped, not blunt The hold is scoped to the structural-frame WBS phase that the failed check belongs to — billing for an unrelated, fully-complete phase on the same project still goes through normally. A failed inspection on one part of the job doesn't freeze revenue for the rest of it.

Two install-time bugs fixed during this pass The ECharts CDN asset was declared with an invalid ('include', url) manifest directive instead of a bare URL string, so window.echarts never loaded and both dashboard charts rendered empty. Separately, .sgc-dashboard's position: absolute; inset: 0 escaped past its position: static parent to the viewport's top-left corner, overlapping Odoo's own top navbar. Both are fixed: the manifest now lists the CDN URL directly, and .o_action_manager:has(.sgc-dashboard) is scoped to position: relative so the dashboard correctly fills the action area below the navbar.
