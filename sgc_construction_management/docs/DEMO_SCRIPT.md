# SGC Construction Management — Full Demo Script

> Word-for-word talk track with stage directions. **[ACTION]** = what you click.
> *(Italics)* = how to say it / tone. Total run time ~12-14 minutes.
> Grounded in the live test.sgctech.ai build — every path here is real.

---

## 0 · Before you start (setup, 2 min before the call)

- [ ] Log in, land on **Construction → Dashboard**.
- [ ] Open **Al Noor Tower** project in a second tab (it's the "critical" one — 3 variations, behind schedule).
- [ ] Have ready: an **RA Billing** record, and **Reporting → Statement of Account**.
- [ ] Confirm reports open in AED and print cleanly.

**Mindset:** you're not selling software. You're showing them their own Monday — with the leaks closed. Talk less, let the screen do the work, pause after each "aha."

---

## 1 · Open on the pain, not the product (60 sec)

*(Calm, conversational. Don't share your screen yet — just talk.)*

> "Before I show you anything — can I tell you what I think actually costs
> construction companies money? It's not the big disaster. It's the small
> gaps. The variation that got agreed on site and never made it onto a
> bill. The payment certificate that sat in someone's inbox for a week.
> The project everyone *felt* was slipping, but nobody could prove until
> month-end — when it was already too late to fix."

*(Pause. Then share screen.)*

> "So instead of a feature tour, let me just walk you through a normal
> month, and show you those gaps closing. Sound good?"

---

## 2 · The Dashboard — "see trouble before it costs you" (2 min)

**[ACTION]** Show the **Dashboard**. Don't explain the widgets. Let them look for 3 seconds.

> "This is the first thing your team sees every morning. No reports to run,
> no spreadsheets to open. Just — where do we stand, today."

**[ACTION]** Point to **Critical Alerts** / **Project Health Matrix**.

> "See this? Al Noor Tower — only 28% complete, but nearly three-quarters
> of its timeline is already gone. The system flags that gap in red, *right
> now* — not at month-end. Today, while you can still do something about it."

*(Note: "planned %" here is time-elapsed against the schedule dates — say it as "where it should be by now," not a formal earned-value baseline.)*

*(Pause — let it land. This is the emotional hook.)*

> "How do you spot a project sliding like this today?"

*(Let them answer. Whatever they say — "month-end reports," "site
manager calls me" — nod and reply:)*

> "Right. And by then the delay's already baked in. This just moves that
> moment forward by three weeks."

## 3 · Variations — "capture every change, bill every dirham" (2.5 min)

> "Here's the one that quietly bleeds margin. Scope changes."

**[ACTION]** Open the **Al Noor Tower** project → click the **Variations** smart button (⇄).

> "On this one project alone — three variations. Extra waterproofing,
> a façade upgrade the client asked for, an MEP reroute. Real money:
> the façade alone is AED 642,000."

**[ACTION]** Click **New** *(it opens already set to Variation Order)*.

> "And logging a new one is this fast. The site engineer agrees a change,
> opens this, types what it is and the amount — done in under a minute.
> It's captured the moment it happens, not three weeks later when nobody
> remembers the number."

**[ACTION]** Close without saving. Back to project → **Print → VO Register**.

> "And here's the payoff: every variation on the project, totalled, on one
> certificate you can hand to the client or the consultant. Nothing slips
> through. Nothing walks off the job unbilled."

*(Objection bait — pause:)*

> "Be honest — how many variations do you think get agreed on site each
> month and never make it onto an invoice?"

## 4 · Billing — "a week of work becomes one click" (2.5 min)

> "Now the part your QS dreads — interim billing."

**[ACTION]** Open an **RA Billing** record → **Print → RA Billing Certificate**.

> "Normally this is a day, maybe two — rebuilding the numbers in a
> spreadsheet every cycle. Here it's already calculated. Previous billed,
> this period, retention, net payable — and it prints as a clean
> certificate, in AED, ready to send. One click."

**[ACTION]** Then **Reporting → Statement of Account** → open a customer → **Print → Statement of Account**.

> "And when the client asks 'where do we stand on payments?' — you don't
> go digging. Full statement of account, running balance, instantly."

> "Faster certificates mean faster cash. That's not a feature — that's
> your cash flow."

## 5 · The forward view — "know where it'll land" (1.5 min)

**[ACTION]** On a project → **Print → Cost-to-Complete**, then **Print → Cash Flow Forecast**.

> "Most systems tell you what you've *spent*. The question that actually
> matters is what this project will *finish* at. Cost-to-complete,
> projected margin, cash flow forecast — the numbers your bank and your
> board ask for, without anyone staying late to build them."

## 6 · The close — "a day in the life" (90 sec)

*(Slow down. This is the whole pitch in four sentences.)*

> "So let me play back the month we just walked through.
> **Morning** — you open the dashboard and see the one project that needs
> you. **On site** — a variation's agreed, your engineer logs it in thirty
> seconds. **Mid-month** — you raise the billing and the certificate prints
> itself. **Month-end** — leadership sees the real position, in one report."

*(Pause.)*

> "Notice what we *didn't* do. No spreadsheets. No chasing inboxes. No
> rebuilding numbers at 9pm. It's the same work your team already does —
> just without the leaks."

*(Then, quietly:)*

> "What would it be worth to you to catch even one slipping project, or one
> unbilled variation, a month earlier?"

*(Stop talking. Let them respond.)*

## 7 · Objection handling

> **Rule:** agree first, then reframe. Never argue. Every objection is
> really "help me feel safe about this." Lower the risk.

### "It looks complicated / my team isn't technical."
> *(Warm, reassuring.)*
> "Totally fair — and that's exactly why I kept this to four screens.
> Your engineer logs a variation the same way they'd send a WhatsApp.
> The complexity is under the hood; the daily use is a button. Want me to
> have your least technical person try logging one, right now?"

### "We already have a system / we use Excel."
> "And Excel is brilliant — until two people edit the same file, or a
> variation lives in someone's head instead of the sheet. The difference
> here isn't features, it's that nothing depends on someone remembering.
> Keep Excel for what it's good at; let this catch the leaks it can't."

### "How much does it cost? / It's expensive."
> *(Reframe to the leak, not the price.)*
> "Let me answer that with a question — one unbilled variation a month,
> how much is that? On Al Noor it was AED 642,000 for a single change.
> The system pays for itself the first time it catches one. Let's look at
> what your numbers actually are."

### "We don't have time to implement / change now."
> "I hear that — everyone's flat out. That's the point, though: this isn't
> a new process on top of your day, it replaces the spreadsheet shuffle
> you're already doing. We start with one project, not the whole company.
> Two weeks, one site, you decide from there."

### "What if the data's wrong / we don't trust the numbers?"
> "Good — you *should* be skeptical of any system's numbers. So here's the
> deal: everything traces back to a source. The VO register is just the
> variations your team entered. The billing is your contract figures.
> Nothing's invented — it just stops things from falling through."

### "Does it handle [specific feature X]?"
> *(Never bluff.)*
> "Honestly — let me show you exactly what it does today rather than
> promise. If X isn't there yet, I'll tell you straight, and we'll note it.
> What's driving the need for X — what problem are you solving with it?"

### "Let me think about it."
> *(Don't push. Pin the next step.)*
> "Of course — it's a real decision. Can I make it concrete? Let's put
> *your* numbers on one of your real projects this week. Thinking about it
> with your own data beats thinking about it from memory. Which project
> should we use?"

## 9 · Do / Don't

**Do:** pause after each pain. Ask questions. Use their project names.
Let silence do the closing.

**Don't:** open menus you won't use. Read every field. Demo features they
didn't ask about. Talk over the "aha" moment.
